Nice Mahjong Solitaire Instruction Guide

By Nice Game Studio

How to Play Mahjong Solitaire

Introduction

Mahjong Solitaire is a single-player adaptation of a centuries old multiplayer game. Also known to some as Shanghai or Taipei, Mahjong is a table-top game that uses a set of 144 tiles. Traditionally, Mahjong is a multiplayer game, where players take turns trying to find matches of tiles to remove from the board. Nice Mahjong Solitaire takes this idea and applies it as a single-player experience, on your phone or tablet devices.

Rules and Objective

Mahjong Solitaire is played by selecting matching tiles and removing them from the board. The goal is to remove all tiles from the board.

There are rules about which tiles you can remove from the board at any given time. Only “free” tiles are eligible to be removed. A “free” tile is a tile that meets the following requirements:

  • No other tiles stacked on top of it
  • Cannot have a tile to the left AND right of it

If a tile meets these requirements, then it is eligible to be selected and removed from the board. Of course, you must find two matching tiles that are both “free”.

In Nice Mahjong Solitaire, you can earn stars for beating a level. You can earn up to three stars in each level. The requirements for earning stars is as follows:

  • Clear the board in less than 10:00 minutes
  • Clear the board without using any hints
  • Clear the board without using any shuffles.

Tips and Tricks

In general, you want to try and make sure that every time you make a match, you “free” up more tiles. The more tiles you can free up with one match, the more chance you have of finishing the board.

As you go along making matches, another general tip is to try and make matches from tiles that are at the top of a stack. You want to try and shrink stacks of tiles down as quickly as possible, otherwise you run the risk of running out of matches because they’re all at the bottom of a stack and you have no way of getting down there.

If you end up running out of matches, you will be given a notification indicating that no more matches are left. Whenever this happens, you have a few options available. You can use the “Undo” button to go back in history until a suitable number of matches become available again. You can also use the “Shuffle” button to mix up the tiles and potentially reveal more matches this way. Lastly, there’s the option to simply “Restart” the board from the beginning.

All of the boards in Nice Mahjong Solitaire are solvable, because they are built in reverse. This is akin to someone taking the tiles by hand and laying them out, one match at a time, until all of the tiles have been laid out. This guarantees that every time a board is laid out, there will always be at least 1 way to solve it. You may discover that there are multiple ways to solve a puzzle. When in doubt, use the undo button until you have a better set of choices.
